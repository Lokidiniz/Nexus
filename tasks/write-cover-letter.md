## Cover Letter Structure

```
[City, Date]

Dear [Editor-in-Chief name if known, else "Editors of [Journal Name]"],

PARAGRAPH 1 — Submission statement
"We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled '[TITLE]' for consideration
for publication in [Journal Name]. This work [1 sentence: what it reports]."

PARAGRAPH 2 — Why this journal (2-3 sentences)
"This work aligns with [Journal Name]'s focus on [scope area] and specifically
advances [theme]. We believe our findings will be of interest to your readership
because [specific connection to journal audience]."

PARAGRAPH 3 — Contribution summary (3-4 sentences)
"In this study, we [objective]. We show that [main result with key number].
Importantly, [what makes this novel or significant]. This [contribution]
addresses a gap in [field]."

PARAGRAPH 4 — Declarations (required by most journals)
"This manuscript has not been published and is not under consideration elsewhere.
All authors have approved the manuscript and agreed to its submission.
[If applicable: This study was approved by [ethics committee, protocol number].]
The authors declare no conflict of interest."

PARAGRAPH 5 — Suggested reviewers (optional, but recommended)
"We suggest the following reviewers based on their expertise in [field]:
- [Name], [Institution] ([email])
- [Name], [Institution] ([email])
We have no objection to any qualified reviewer."

Sincerely,
[Corresponding author full name]
[Position and Institution]
[Email]
[ORCID: if available]
```

---

## Tone Rules

- Professional and confident, not boastful
- Never: "This is a groundbreaking study..." (editor decides that)
- Never: "We hope you will consider..." (weak)
- Always: "We are pleased to submit..." (standard, not weak)
- Avoid: listing all authors in the body (signature is sufficient)

---

## Journal-Specific Adaptations

**Elsevier journals**: typically require ethical statement in cover letter if human/animal subjects involved.

**Nature/Science family**: cover letter is critical — 1 paragraph max per section, editor reads 100s per week.

**Brazilian journals (ABNT)**: formal PT-BR tone, include Lattes links if requested by journal.

**Open access**: mention if article processing charge (APC) waiver is requested (if applicable).
